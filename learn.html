semicolon ; - ESSENTIALLY A PERIOD. Semicolons are used to combine two or more independent clauses into one sentence. Examples: I went to the grocery store; I bought oranges that were on sale. The tiger and the penguin are both unique in their coloring; both animals are wonderful to look at. Special rule: A connecting word can seperate the two sentences followed by a comma Examples: I went to the grocery store; therefore, I spent all my money on oranges. The tiger and the penguin are both unique in their coloring; however, both animals are wonderful to look at.
colon : - A COMPLETE SENTENCE:EXAMPLE A colon introduces an example based on the information before the colon. Examples: You know what to do: practice. You may be required to bring many things: sleeping bags, pans, utensils, and warm clothing. I want the following items: butter, sugar, and flour. I need an assistant who can do the following: input data, write reports, and complete tax forms.
comma , - 1) Commas are used to separate things in a list Example: When Jimmy went to the zoo, he saw a lion, a tiger, and a penguin. 2) Commas are also used to separate two independent clauses when a conjuntion is used Example: Jimmy wondered why there was a penguin in the tiger cage, and he decided to ask the zookeeper if the penguin was in the correct place. 3) Commas are used between an independent clause and a dependent clause Example: As jimmy was looking for the zookeeper, he noticed the penguin waddle away and jump into its own cage. 4) Commas are used to separate a non-restictive (non-essential) part of the sentence. Example: The penguin, which was black and white in color, seemed to be very content with its life in the zoo.
