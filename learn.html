semicolon ;

ESSENTIALLY A PERIOD. Semicolons are used to combine two or more independent clauses into one sentence. Examples: I went to the grocery store; I bought oranges that were on sale. The tiger and the penguin are both unique in their coloring; both animals are wonderful to look at. Special rule: A connecting word can seperate the two sentences followed by a comma Examples: I went to the grocery store; therefore, I spent all my money on oranges. The tiger and the penguin are both unique in their coloring; however, both animals are wonderful to look at.
